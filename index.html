Who We Serve

Quartet of the
Vulnerable

01
Low-Income Families
Over 39.8% of Korean elderly live in poverty—#1 in the OECD. We provide essential resources, dignity-centered care, and pathways toward self-sufficiency.
02
Children Without Parents
~329,000 children in US foster care. 78% experience basic needs insecurity. We provide mentorship, education, and sustained care so no child feels forgotten.
03
Widows
Widows lose up to 75% of their support systems overnight. ~700,000 women are widowed each year. We restore community belonging and build financial independence.
04
Immigrant Refugees
Immigrants face a 20.9% poverty rate vs 9.9% for non-immigrants. We ease transitions, create networks of opportunity, and champion the dignity of every newcomer.

Our Story

About
Tessera

Tessera Compassion Foundation began not with a plan, but with a person. In high school, founder Aiden Kim met an unhoused elderly woman who declined his monetary donation and instead explained her need for medication for herself and her son.

That encounter profoundly shaped his understanding of compassion—and ultimately led him to pursue pharmacy and community outreach work in underserved cities like Stockton and Compton. Believing in the power of storytelling, he used $10,000 of his personal savings to travel to Korea, where simple videos providing rest and appreciation for elderly living in poverty gradually evolved into a global movement.

Today, Tessera Compassion Foundation is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit dedicated to restoring dignity, promoting self-sustainability, and serving vulnerable communities through compassionate action—one story, one person, one tessera at a time.

About the Founder
Aiden Kim
Licensed Pharmacist · Founder & President, TCF

In high school, Aiden Kim met an unhoused elderly woman who declined his monetary donation—and instead explained her need for medication. That single encounter reshaped his understanding of compassion and set him on a path toward pharmacy and community service.

After years of medical outreach in underserved communities in Stockton and Compton, he used $10,000 of his personal savings to travel to Korea. What began as simple videos—providing rest, appreciation, and visibility to elderly individuals living in poverty—gradually evolved into a viral movement reaching millions.

Guided by the belief that social media could be a force for genuine human connection rather than spectacle, Aiden founded Tessera Compassion Foundation in 2025. Under his leadership, TCF operates with a clear voice: warm, honest, and never exploitative—restoring dignity one story at a time.
